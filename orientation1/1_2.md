(W) Take a real-world operational system that can be observed (bank, supermarket) and identify the elements associated with it in terms of variability, complexity, interconnectedness.


- Customer amounts and their needs vary. Some need quick services while others require longer consultations. Staff availability may also vary.
- Customer waiting time depends on the number of tellers, service speed, queue length and arrival rate. If one teller is slow, the whole queue can grow.
- The system becomes complex when different customer types, different service needs, lunch breaks, peak hours and other human factors interact. Small changes can lead to big effects.
