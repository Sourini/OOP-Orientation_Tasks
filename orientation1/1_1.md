(W) Think of everyday situations that simulation could analyze. What aspects of the situation make the simulation approach specifically appropriate for analyzing the situation (i.e., think about and explain why simulation would be specifically better than experimenting in the real world). What aspects of sustainability can you incorporate into the simulation (see, for example, chapter 1).


- A supermarket checkout can be simulated to study customer waiting times, cashier utilization, queue lengths, and the effect of opening or closing checkout counters. 
- Simulation is appropriate because experimenting in the real store could disturb customers, increase labor costs, and produce inconsistent conditions.
- Simulation could include energy use of checkout machines, time waste caused by slow service, and staff workload.
